FAQs

Common questions.

Everything you need to know before booking.

How much does a new fence cost?
It depends on the length, height, post type and access. As a rough guide, a standard 6ft closeboard fence with concrete posts runs around £80–£110 per metre, supply and install. We always provide a clear, itemised quote before any work starts.
Do you remove the old fence?
Yes. We remove all old fencing, posts and concrete as part of the job. Everything is taken away and disposed of properly — you won't have to deal with any of it.
What timber do you use?
We use pressure-treated softwood on every job. It's treated with preservative that protects against rot, insect damage and fungal decay. It lasts significantly longer than untreated timber.
Do you supply the materials?
Yes. We supply all materials — timber, concrete, gravel boards, fixtures. Everything is included in our quote. You don't need to source anything yourself.
How long does a fencing job take?
A typical residential fence replacement (10–20 metres) takes 1–2 days. Larger jobs or jobs with access issues may take longer. We'll give you a clear timeline with the quote.
Are you insured?
Yes. We carry £2 million public liability insurance. We can provide a copy of our certificate on request.
Do you work in the rain?
We can work in light rain, but heavy rain or waterlogged ground can affect the quality of the finished job. If we need to reschedule, we'll let you know as early as possible.
Do you offer guarantees?
Yes. We stand behind our work. If there's an issue with workmanship, we'll come back and sort it at no extra cost.
Can you match my neighbour's fence?
In most cases, yes. Closeboard fencing is a standard construction method, so matching existing panels is usually straightforward. We'll discuss this during the quote.
Do I need planning permission for a fence?
Standard garden fences up to 2 metres don't usually require planning permission. Fences next to a highway must be under 1 metre. We'll advise you if your situation is different.
